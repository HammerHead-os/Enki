02 - The Playbook

But What is Lobbying?

Lobbying is the act of trying to influence government decisions - by hiring professionals who testify at hearings and push for or against specific bills.

It's legal, common and very effective.

At its peak, the Internet Association deployed up to 22 lobbyists and spent $2.4 million in 2017 alone. In Illinois, those lobbyists testified against bill after bill.

Behind every "oppose" in the dataset is a real person in a real hearing room — trying to kill the bills that would give you control over your own data.
